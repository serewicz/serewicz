# Case Study: Founder-Led SaaS Acquisition Target

## Scenario

A founder-led B2B SaaS company is being evaluated as an acquisition target. The company has meaningful customer traction, but technology operations depend heavily on the founder and a small number of senior engineers.

## Problem

The diligence review identifies founder dependency, manual deployments, limited documentation, informal security ownership, and integration readiness risk.

These are not just technical issues. They create business risk during acquisition integration because the acquirer needs predictable knowledge transfer, reliable deployment practices, and clear operational ownership.

## Repositories Used

| Repository | Role |
|---|---|
| [Executive AI Advisor](https://github.com/serewicz/Executive-AI-Advisor) | Analyzes diligence materials and generates cited technology risk outputs. |
| [CTO Operating System](https://github.com/serewicz/cto-operating-system) | Provides diligence methodology, risk scoring, and 100-day planning framework. |
| [K8s Platform Blueprint](https://github.com/serewicz/k8s-platform-blueprint) | Provides remediation reference patterns for platform governance, deployment controls, and evidence. |
| [Engineering Operating Metrics](https://github.com/serewicz/engineering-operating-metrics) | Measures post-close delivery flow, review quality, rework, cost, and governance. |

## Operating Workflow

1. Upload diligence documents into Executive AI Advisor.
2. Generate a Technology Due Diligence Report.
3. Use CTO Operating System to structure risks, owners, and board questions.
4. Map deployment and platform risks to K8s Platform Blueprint control patterns.
5. Use Engineering Operating Metrics after close to track whether execution is improving.

## Outputs

- Technology Due Diligence Report
- 100-Day Acquisition Integration Plan
- Risk Heatmap
- Board Questions

## Example Board Questions

- Which production responsibilities currently depend on the founder or one senior engineer?
- What deployment processes must be documented before close?
- Which integration risks could interrupt customer operations?
- What should management complete in the first 30 days post-close?

## Business Value

The case study shows how diligence findings can become a practical acquisition integration plan. It connects risk identification, operating methodology, remediation patterns, and post-close measurement.
