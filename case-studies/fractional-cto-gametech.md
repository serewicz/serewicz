# Fractional CTO Engagement

## Situation

An early-stage gaming company needed experienced technology leadership while the founder was still defining the product, architecture, and team model. The company was not ready for a full-time CTO but needed disciplined technology decision-making.

## Challenge

The founder needed guidance on operating system decisions, networking architecture, infrastructure direction, initial hiring strategy, ADRs, process documentation, and technology planning. The work needed to reduce future technology risk without overbuilding too early.

## Approach

I advised the founder as a fractional CTO, helping frame decisions around scalability, operational complexity, hiring sequence, documentation, and architecture tradeoffs. The engagement emphasized clear decision records, practical planning, and foundations that could support future product and team growth.

## Outcome

The engagement created more disciplined technology planning and clearer decision context for the founder. It helped establish scalable foundations while preserving flexibility appropriate for an early-stage company.

## Business Relevance

For CEOs and founders, this shows how experienced technology leadership can reduce risk before a full executive hire is justified. For boards and investors, it demonstrates how early technology choices can be made with better governance and clearer tradeoffs. For growth-stage companies, it shows the value of decision discipline before scale pressure increases.

## Key Themes

Fractional CTO, founder advisory, architecture decisions, infrastructure planning, documentation, hiring strategy, technology risk reduction.
