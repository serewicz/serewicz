# Linux Foundation Transformation

## Situation

From 2012 to 2026, I held technology leadership roles at The Linux Foundation, including Vice President, Technology & Education from 2024 to 2026. The organization supported one of the world's largest open-source education ecosystems serving hundreds of thousands of learners, developers, and enterprise users.

## Challenge

The environment required technology strategy, platform modernization, AI initiatives, cybersecurity programs, acquisition integration, executive alignment, and distributed team leadership. The work had to support scale, reliability, governance, and business alignment across a complex open-source ecosystem.

## Approach

I led technology strategy and transformation work across platforms, programs, teams, and executive stakeholders. The focus was aligning technology decisions with organizational outcomes, improving governance, supporting modernization, integrating acquired capabilities, and enabling distributed teams to operate with clearer direction.

## Outcome

The work supported technology transformation, platform evolution, AI adoption, cybersecurity maturity, acquisition integration, and executive alignment across a large open-source education ecosystem.

## Business Relevance

For CEOs, this reflects technology leadership at organizational scale. For boards, it shows experience connecting technology strategy, governance, and risk. For PE firms, it demonstrates transformation and integration experience. For growth-stage companies, it shows how operating models, platforms, and teams must evolve as scale and complexity increase.

## Key Themes

Technology leadership, platform modernization, AI initiatives, cybersecurity, acquisition integration, governance, distributed teams, business alignment.
